Deconstructing Modern Cyber Threats: A Practical Perspective on Privacy, Isolation, and Threat Mitigation for Personal Systems

July 16, 2025

Safwan Nasir

Isolation, and Threat Mitigation for Personal Systems

Author

As individual targets of cyber threats are on the rise, individuals with little explicit advice for personal security are at a loss. The majority of academic literature on cybersecurity continues to focus on enterprise security models and algorithm development relevant to security. The community of users with no clear evidence of how to best secure or defend their day-to-day use of technology must navigate a complex and inconsistent array of tools to mitigate risk, if they do not simply abandon the effort altogether. This paper provides a hands-on evaluation of personal cyber security tools and strategies and is presented as a 'best effort' study, that does not attempt new machine learning models or experimental applications. Built on structured threat modeling and understanding the attack surfaces of our daily computing activities, the examination of tools across Windows, macOS, and Linux include: (operating systems) hardening; (applications) sandboxing, encryption, virtualization, and privacy-enhancement, among others. It is not implied that these are the best tools available to the user, rather descriptive comparisons of tools based on criteria such as usability, effectiveness, and sustainability. Although it is recognized that individual users find practical solutions conducive to the activity or job in which they are engaged, document-based communication for a review of tools such as VeraCrypt, Qubes OS, Tor, and DNS filtering will generate new insights which, hopefully, will offer actionable options for programs and users appearing in this paper including post-secondary students, investigative journalists, digital developers and activists.

Real-Time Student Residence Identity Verification System Using Vision Transformers with Automated Alert Integration and Edge Optimization

November 2023 – June 2024

RIT Intel CoE Lab with Dr. Sinthuja Muthurishnan

Lead Researcher and Author

  • Researched existing identity verification systems, edge-based face recognition frameworks, and alerting mechanisms to identify limitations in speed, accuracy, and automation for student residence security environments.
  • Designed and implemented a facial recognition system using OpenCV and a pre-trained CNN with HOG features, applying image preprocessing (resizing, normalization, grayscale) to optimize processing time and achieve 96.5% identification accuracy.
  • Integrated Firebase Realtime Database for secure access logging and Twilio API for automated SMS alerts on unauthorized entries and student absences beyond curfew hours (e.g., 10 PM), enhancing real-time responsiveness.
  • Conducted robustness testing under varying lighting conditions, facial expressions, and entry scenarios, and benchmarked performance against manual identity tracking systems to demonstrate reductions in entry errors and administrative workload.

DermAssist: A Hybrid Vision Transformer System for Skin Lesion Diagnosis with Automated Alerting and Dual-Sided Portals

February 2025 – June 2025

RIT Embedded Systems Lab with Dr. Shruthi G

Lead Researcher and Author

  • Outlined the importance of early detection and compared conventional diagnostic methods with technology-assisted systems for identifying skin cancer types such as BCC, SCC, melanoma, and Merkel Cell Carcinoma, etc.
  • Reviewed major skin lesion datasets and trained a multi-class classifier using CNN and BEiT Vision Transformer models on DermNet, ISIC, and HAM10000, with preprocessing and augmentation to enhance lesion visibility.
  • Developed a lesion analysis pipeline using U-Net for segmentation, ResNet for feature extraction, and incorporating ABCDE (Asymmetry, Border, Color, Diameter, Evolving) rule-based shape, color, and texture features.
  • Studied Grad-CAM and SHAP techniques for model interpretability and reviewed dataset-specific studies, highlighting challenges such as false positives and limited external validation.
  • Identified key limitations such as segmentation accuracy, generalization, and computational cost, and outlined future directions including multimodal integration and low/zero-shot learning for rare condition detection.
  • Developed a complete Dermatology Diagnosis Assistant that generates diagnostic reports and auto-alerts dermatologists via Twilio API for high-confidence cases (>90%).
  • Implemented HIPAA-compliant, encrypted portals using Streamlit (patient) and Flask (doctor), with AWS S3 for secure data storage.

EdgeViT++: Efficient Vision Transformers for Edge Devices via Dynamic Token Pruning and Hybrid Quantization

December 2024 – June 2025

Undergraduate Student Researcher

Lead Researcher and Author

  • Explored the key challenges in deploying standard ViT models on edge hardware, such as quadratic self-attention, uniform token processing, lack of early exits, and memory inefficiency, emphasized their impact on real-time inference and latency.
  • Surveyed recent literature on efficient ViT design, including token pruning, quantization-aware training (QAT), hybrid CNN-transformer architectures, and AutoML-based optimization strategies, identifying gaps in unified, latency-aware solutions.
  • Proposed the EdgeViT++ conceptual framework — combining dynamic token pruning, hybrid CNN-ViT structures, and 4-bit quantization — to theoretically enhance performance under hardware and energy constraints without retraining.
  • Analyzed mathematical formulations including entropy-based token saliency, adaptive masking via learnable Gumbel-based strategies, memory footprint modeling, and Pareto-front multi-objective trade-offs for inference-time optimization.
  • Benchmarked theoretical comparisons of EdgeViT++ with existing models (e.g., LeViT, DeiT, EfficientFormer), identifying strengths in terms of accuracy-latency balance, VRAM consumption, and deployment compatibility on Jetson devices.
  • Explored practical deployment techniques such as ONNX graph simplification, TensorRT integration, hardware-specific quantization tuning, and lightweight knowledge distillation approaches.
  • Discussed broader implications of efficient ViT design, including improving model sustainability, supporting real-time edge AI, and making deep learning more accessible on resource-constrained devices.
